I’m Efe Sakarya, a rising junior at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) pursuing a Computer Science degree. My main focus is using machine learning to solve real-life problems.

At Stanford's Vasanawala Group, I work on constructing an Implicit Neural Representation (INR) model that will efficiently reconstruct a final image from an undersampled k-space data (the raw, un-imaged digital signal collected by the scanner's parallel coils). What this means is that instead of longer runs inside an MRI machine that might lead to motion artifacts overtime caused by fatigues or even some uncontrolled movements that are seen mainly in pediatric patients, as a group, we take shorter data in the MRI machine to prevent such motion-related issues. What we aim to do is then reconstructing these images using machine learning and neural representation pipelines as efficiently (in terms of memory and time) and accurate as possible. 

At Caltech’s Orphan Lab, I developed a machine learning pipeline for analyzing Total Organic Carbon (TOC) and DNA sequencing data. Using Random Forest regression, I model relationships between environmental conditions and algal productivity to optimize carbon sequestration efficiency — research supported by the Kiyo and Eiko Tomiyasu SURF Scholarship.
